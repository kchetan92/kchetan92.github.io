WASD: The Indoor Navigation Solution

Problem Statement

GPS navigation has become a common place thanks to Google maps. We can now predict how to go from point A to B, even if its a new neighborhood. They now have maps for malls and airports as well.

However, the same interface doesn't scale well for indoor navigation

The project has been named WASD to show

My Role

Researcher, UX Designer (solo project)

UX Skills

Sketching, storyboarding, wireframing, prototyping, user testing

Platform

Mobile

Duration

Fall 2017

Tools

Adobe Illustrator, InVision

Goals

Rethink navigation in a 3 dimensional space. Analyze what’s most important to a casual shopper visiting a mall.

Research

The purpose of this research excercise was to identify the variety of problems which people face while navigating multi story buildings.

Some structures have an ill reputation for being confusing. There can be a variety of reasons for that. My college building is one of such structures. Its been a whole semester, and I still sometimes lose my way in the building.

Casual conversations revealed that I am not the only one who feels that way. But interviewing them “why or how” they feel about walking through a building did not reveal much information. Instead I asked them to draw a floor map of a building they frequent the most. Analyzing these pictures gave me an idea of what people observe and remember about their surroundings. Most of the participants missed the doors and hallways which they don't frequent.

Personas

Based on the interview responses, I created some personas based on my understanding of different categories of people in this context.

Experience map

Based on my observations, I constructed the following experience map. It helped me identify the pain points at each phase of a user's journey and observe his/her emotions. This sets a theme or defines the requirements for the UI.

Competitors

Google maps is one of the most popular apps for vehicle navigation. Combined with the vast amount of data that Google has, they're your safest bet when exploring an unknown territory. The indoor maps feature in Google maps now features major structures such as airports and malls.

Some other companies such as insoft, accuware, NavVis, IndoorAtlas, use technology such as WiFi positions, bluetooth beacons, vision based navigation. Although these technologies overcome GPS's inaccuracy indoors, they're similar to Google Maps in terms of user experience.

Storyboarding

The next step towards identifying the solution is sketching out some possible ideas. I sketched around 8 different solutions (diverge) before I could arrive (converge) at one idea that I would be working on.

Low-fi Prototype

The groundwork setup till this stage is used to create the low fidelity prototype. Reasonable rough sketches of the final interface were made with well developed interaction strategy. The main purpose of the low-fi prototype is to quickly generate some sketches so that it can be tested with some users.

Usability Testing

Testing the prototype with a volunteers revealed that

  • Users didn't recognize some icons. Better icons or helper text might help.

  • Some of the initial loading screens were confusing. The user didn't know how to proceed. Most of the loading can be done in the background.
