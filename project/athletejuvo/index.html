AthleteJuvo

Gamification for Athlete Rehabilitation Exercises and Social Support

My Role

UX Researcher & Interaction Designer

UX Skills

Contextual Inquiry, Interviewing, Brainstorming, Wireframing, Prototyping, Affinity Mapping

Duration

4 months (Fall 2017)

Team

Yu-Yin Shen, Shih-Ting Lin, Yi Lee, Chetan Keshav

PROBLEM

When athletes get injured

Daily practice, demanding training routine and interacting with fellow teammates and coach is an essential part of an athletes' social life. But when they get injured, they are unable to participate in these activities. Common symptoms include depression, loss of identity and isolation. The rehabilitation process is slow and frustrating.

RESEARCH

Literature Review

The team had a rich background in psychology and thanks to their expertise we were able to assimilate a good number of popular literature available on the psychological well being of injured athletes. Initial survey also included some interviews with friends of the team members who have been through similar ordeal.

Survey

Based on what we had discovered so far, we designed a survey to validate our assumptions and gain useful insights. We contacted multiple athlete groups in the University of Michigan to participate in the online survey on hosted on Surveycake.

Of the 87 athletes who had faced serious injury, 92% reported suffering from isolation and depression.

Interviewing the experts

To get an overview of the process, we contacted some experts in the field. Interviews from physical therapist at the University of Michigan, mental coach and admin of popular Facbook groups dealing with well being of injured athletes gave us an overview of what athletes go through during the recovery period. We learnt that athhletes often do not complete the rehabilitation process.

Interviewing the athletes

Some of the participants of the online interview agreed to meet us for a face to face interview. An interview protocal was created based on expert interview and results from the survey. Their tales of injury and recovery gave helped us empathize with them and live the experience.

We learnt that they had a tough time keeping track of their rehabilitation process since it is slow and progress is hard to notice. Some athletes who had sufferred serious injuries had a tough time coping with the fact that they might not be able to participate for a very long time.

I’m definitely a competitive person. My whole life has been that way, so it’s definitely translating to my personality
Nobody truly understands what you’re going through, It’s a lonely process. Fully recovering from an injury is 90% percent mental, 10% physical.
Injuries made me feel at times like I wasn't a part of the team. And I was a captain.
Most of my social interaction came through my sport; without being able to go ride a bike with my friends, I was pretty lonely.

RESEARCH ANALYSIS

Affinity wall

We created an affinity wall to discover trends from the information we had so faron an online whiteboard called realtimeboard. It allows real time collabration, has a simple UI and supports exporting in PDF. It was very easy to tag the notes and move them (as a group or individually) around the board.

Personas

We found athletes at varying stages of recovery and varying severity of the injury. We decided to focus on two broad categories and made two personas to go with it.

Tammy is a junior and a captain of the college rugby team. During one of the pre season practice she sufferred from a ligament tear. She hopes to recover as soon as possible.

Jason is in the varsity rowing team. He was in a severe car crash a few months ago and is currently recovering. He has been unable to participate in training sessions since then. He has been recommended a lengthy rehabilitation process. He is very depressed and isolated and seems to be losing faith in the rehabilitation excercises as he cannot see his progress.

IDEAS

Requirements

The design innovation process guides the designers to follow a path of research, analysis, synthesis and realization. But this process is rarely observed in order.

We went through several iterations of the process and decided that it we should focus on the following aspects

  1. Bond injured athletes Varsities with sufficient student population can have a critical mass of injured students to form a community. Engaging them in a community allows them to empathize with each other.

  2. Track excercise progress This ability allows us to gamify the rehabilitation process. We can achieve some reliability in the tracking process by asking users to use a tracking device during workout.

  3. Has to be fun! The success of the solution lies in its popularity. It should be fun and engaging.

How might we simplify the rehabilitation exercises?

Now that we had the background research and personas ready, we thought of some ideas to simplify the rehabilitation excercise. A solution which could detect the counts of excercises being performed. We considered several technnologies such as Kinect and ECG devices. But they lacked mobility or accuracy. We decided to use stretchable sensors to detect motion.

SOLUTION

AthleteJuvo

AthleteJuvo aims at connecting the injured athlete to the society. Keep them involed and around other people. It is a mobile application to gamify, visualize and socialize the rehabilitation process.

Design Decisions

The app is aimed to rekindle the competition spirit. So the first screen highlights the current standing with the current team followed by individual contribution and then lists the challanges or excercises for the day.

Team spirit echoes in the achievement tab where the team's achievement is focused. We were very careful not to demotivate the injured athlete in any situation.

Apologies to OSU for such illustration.

The game allows the athletes to invite their "fit" teammates to contribute to the score. This helps them communicate and bond with their original team.

The athlete can track his/her progress in the dedicated section of the app. The goals are set by the physical therapist.

The athlete is educated of the right way to excercise using a simple illustration. Pending repetition of the excercise is also shown.

Learning

This project has been a wonderful learning experince for me. Especially because of my teammates. They are from a completely different background, and we had complementary skill sets. The team was hardworking and their diligence kept me on my toes.
